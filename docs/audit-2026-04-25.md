## The core asymmetry

We've built a **production-quality engineering chassis with a 2024-era AI brain inside it.** The chassis is the easy part. Buffett-grade reasoning is the hard part, and most of what makes Opus 4.7 special isn't on the field yet.

## Six upgrade levers — 6.5 → 8.5

Ordered by leverage, not effort.

1. **Extended thinking + parallel tool calls in the wake loop.** Opus 4.7's `thinking` blocks let it reason about a position for 5-30 seconds before deciding. Parallel tool calls let it research VOO + SCHD + V + MA simultaneously instead of one at a time. Both are SDK config changes — maybe a half-day each. The reasoning quality jumps materially.
2. **Post-mortem learning loop.** Agent reads its own past trades from `Trade` + `BrainEntry`, asks "where was I wrong?" and writes correction entries with `supersededById` pointing at the prior thesis. This is the unique advantage of having a versioned brain — and we don't use it. Maybe 2-3 days. Without this, the agent makes the same mistakes weekly.
3. **Walk-forward backtest validation.** The backtester currently runs in-sample; we never prove the strategies work out-of-sample. A walk-forward harness (train on 2015-2020, test on 2021-2023, repeat) would either validate the approach or expose it. This is the single biggest credibility gap. ~3 days.
4. **SnapTrade read-only sync.** Real Robinhood / Schwab portfolios appear in the app. No execution yet, but the agent reasons over actual holdings. Closes the "I want to see what it does with MY portfolio" loop. ~2 days.
5. **Live market data feed.** Alpaca SIP ($99/mo) replaces the 15-min IEX feed. Required for any "oracle" claim — value investing tolerates delay, but the product loses credibility when prices are visibly stale.
6. **Adversarial split-call meetings.** Two separate Anthropic calls debate the same decision (one bullish, one bearish), then a third synthesizes. Real disagreement instead of one-shot generated transcripts. The current meetings are theater; this would be substance. ~2 days.

## Three structural moats to deepen

Ordered by depth.

- **The brain as compounding IP.** Every wake should read the brain, every wake should write to it, every meeting should consolidate. After a year, your brain is a private trove of investment doctrine + observed mistakes — that's not reproducible. We have the schema; we underuse it.
- **The audit trail as the regulatory wedge.** If we ever go real-money, the `GovernorDecision` table is the single best piece of compliance posture in a small shop — every refusal has a structured reason, a Mandate version, and a governor version. That trail alone could be the basis of a "transparent, rule-driven autonomous investing" registration vs. an "AI black box" one.
- **The personality + comic format.** Sounds frivolous; it isn't. Berkshire's letters are the moat for Berkshire — the *voice* compounds trust. Meeting-as-comic-strip is a generational UX bet, and nobody else is making it.

## What stands between us and "Berkshire takes notice"

- 18 months of walk-forward validation on real-time data
- Demonstrated outperformance vs. SPY net of cost
- A way to invest real money (SnapTrade execution, or a partnership with a registered broker-dealer)
- The post-mortem loop running every wake so the agent's reasoning is *visibly improving over time*, not flat
- Public dashboard with the actual numbers (the audit trail makes this credible — most AI funds can't open the books)

## Realistic path

The next 4-6 weeks should concentrate on items **1, 2, 3** from the upgrade lever list (extended thinking, learning loop, walk-forward). Those are the ones that change the *kind* of thing this is. Everything else is incremental.
